The Cyrillic Alphabet

Most find the concept of learning a new language to be challenging,
and the addition of a new alphabet with entirely new sounds and symbols adds
another layer of complexity. However, for anyone who's first language uses a Latin alphabet,
Cyrillic serves as the easiest first step when trying to bridge the gap into a new alphabet

Shared letters

The easiest letters to process as an English Speaker

The fastest letters which any native English Speaker can process are the following:

A, К, M, О, T.

These letters sounds nearly identical to their english counterparts, and can be quickly interpreted.
Words only containing these letters are easily pronouncable for a native English speaker.
In fact some of the first words you will learn when starting Russian will contain these letters.
For example:

Russian English
Мама Mom
кот Cat
Кто? Who?

Silent Letters

These letters effect others!

These final letters don't actually make a noise!
They merely effect the preceding letter, making it either hard or soft!

Russian Letter What does it do?
Ъ Hard sign! This makes the preceding letter more pronounced!
ь Soft sign! This makes the preceding letter less pronounced!
